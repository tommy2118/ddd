Evolving the Shared Language

A Ubiquitous Language must grow and adapt alongside your business and domain understanding. Learn strategies to evolve the language without disrupting systems or workflows.

Why Evolving the Language is Crucial

At Kalkomey, our business domains, such as Certification Management and Compliance, are constantly evolving. Without a strategy for maintaining the shared language:

Inconsistencies Emerge

New terms or concepts may conflict with existing ones, leading to confusion across teams.

Systems Become Outdated

Legacy terms in the codebase may no longer reflect the actual business domain.

Collaboration Suffers

Teams may revert to siloed communication as the shared language loses relevance.

Strategies for Evolving the Shared Language

Regular Language Audits

Schedule periodic reviews of the shared language with stakeholders to identify outdated terms and ensure new ones are well-defined.

  • Are all terms still in use?
  • Do any terms have conflicting definitions?
  • Have new workflows introduced undefined concepts?

Versioned Documentation

Use version control (e.g., Git) for your shared language documentation. This allows teams to track changes and understand historical context. 

# Example: Git commit for language update
git commit -m "Updated 'Certification' definition to include new expiration rules"

Deprecate Carefully

When removing outdated terms, provide clear migration paths for teams and systems. For example: 

# Before
CertificationStatus: active, expired

# After
CertificationState: valid, invalid
# Deprecated 'CertificationStatus' remains temporarily for backwards compatibility.

Collaborate on Changes

Involve domain experts and engineers when making significant changes to the language. Use workshops or collaborative tools to build consensus.

Example: Updating Certification Terminology

Here’s an example of evolving the language for the Certification Management domain: 

# Old terminology
CertificationStatus:
- active
- expired

# New terminology
CertificationState:
- valid
- invalid

# Migration Steps
1. Update all references in the codebase.
2. Notify teams of the change and provide training materials.
3. Deprecate old terms with clear warnings in APIs and schemas.
