Collaboration on Shared Language

A shared Ubiquitous Language thrives on collaboration. Learn best practices for fostering communication and teamwork across Kalkomey’s engineering and business teams.

Why Collaboration is Essential

At Kalkomey, we rely on effective collaboration to ensure that our shared language reflects both technical and business needs. Without collaboration:

Miscommunication Increases

Teams may define terms differently, leading to inconsistencies in the codebase and processes.

Key Perspectives are Missed

Important insights from domain experts or engineers may be overlooked.

Innovation is Stifled

Cross-functional collaboration fosters creativity and alignment on innovative solutions.

Best Practices for Collaboration

Facilitate Regular Workshops

Organize regular workshops with domain experts, product managers, and developers to discuss new terms, refine existing ones, and address ambiguities.

  • Review recently added domain features.
  • Clarify terms causing confusion in current workflows.
  • Document agreed-upon changes immediately.

Use Collaborative Tools

Leverage tools like Confluence, Notion, or Miro to create a centralized repository for the shared language. Ensure all stakeholders have access and encourage contributions.

Adopt Feedback Loops

Collect feedback from engineers and domain experts regularly to understand how the language is used in practice and identify potential improvements.

Encourage Cross-Team Communication

Create opportunities for teams to collaborate, such as joint planning sessions or pairing exercises, ensuring that technical and business perspectives are aligned.

Example: Resolving Ambiguities

In a recent Kalkomey workshop, teams debated the term "applicant." After collaborative discussions, the following distinctions were agreed upon: 

Applicant:
- A user who has started the registration process but has not completed it.

Candidate:
- A user who has completed registration but has not yet passed the required certification test.

Certified User:
- A user who has successfully obtained their certification.
