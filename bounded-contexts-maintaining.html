Maintaining Bounded Contexts

Over time, business needs evolve, and so must your Bounded Contexts. Maintaining well-defined boundaries ensures your system can adapt without introducing unnecessary complexity.

Why Maintenance Matters

Without consistent maintenance, Bounded Contexts can drift from their original purpose, leading to overlapping responsibilities, inconsistent terminology, and unnecessary coupling. Maintenance ensures:

Consistency

Keeps terminology and rules aligned within the context.

Adaptability

Enables smooth integration of new business requirements.

Sustainability

Prevents complexity from growing over time.

Strategies for Maintaining Bounded Contexts

Use these strategies to ensure your contexts remain effective over time:

1. Regularly Review Boundaries

Revisit the boundaries of each context periodically to ensure they still align with current business goals and workflows.

Example: If the LMS expands to handle international certifications, update its boundaries to include new rules and responsibilities.

2. Refactor Contexts Incrementally

If boundaries need adjustment, refactor in small, manageable steps to avoid disruptions.

Example: Splitting a single E-Regulations context into separate contexts for Hunting and Fishing Regulations.

3. Monitor Dependencies

Keep track of how contexts depend on one another. Address cases where dependencies grow too tightly coupled.

Example: Avoid making LMS overly dependent on HuntWise by using shared APIs instead of direct integration.

4. Engage Domain Experts

Work with domain experts to ensure terminology and workflows remain accurate and relevant as the business evolves.

Example: HuntWise Expansion

When Kalkomey expanded HuntWise to include detailed fishing tools, it required redefining boundaries and responsibilities. Here’s how we maintained clarity: 

Original Context: HuntWise
- Responsibilities: Hunting tools (GPS maps, plans, trophies).
- Terminology: Hunter, Hunt Area, Season Plan.

Updated Context: HuntWise
- Responsibilities: Expanded to include fishing tools and seasons.
- Terminology: Angler, Fishing Spot, Trophy Catch, Fishing Season.

Action Taken:
- Updated boundaries to separate hunting-specific and fishing-specific workflows.
- Refactored shared terms (e.g., "Season Plan") into context-specific terms (e.g., "Hunting Plan" and "Fishing Season").

This careful maintenance ensured HuntWise remained flexible and scalable while supporting new business objectives.
