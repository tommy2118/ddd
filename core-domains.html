Core Domains

The Core Domain is the heart of your business. It’s where innovation drives the most value and where your best efforts should be focused. Identifying and prioritizing your Core Domain is a critical step in Strategic Design.

What is a Core Domain?

The Core Domain represents the most critical area of your system, where differentiation and innovation deliver the greatest business value. It is typically:

  • Business-Critical: Directly tied to your company’s success.
  • Unique: Distinct from standard or off-the-shelf solutions.
  • Innovation-Focused: Where your team invests its best talent and resources.

By identifying your Core Domain, you can ensure that your most valuable resources are focused on what matters most.

How to Identify Your Core Domain

Identifying the Core Domain involves analyzing your business objectives, workflows, and competitive differentiators. Here are three steps to guide you:

1. Analyze Business Objectives

Identify the areas of your business that are directly tied to success. Ask, “What part of the system delivers the most value to our customers?”

2. Evaluate Uniqueness

Determine what differentiates your business from competitors. Look for areas where your solution is unique or customized.

3. Focus on Innovation

Highlight areas where innovation drives value. Avoid treating commoditized or generic processes as Core Domains.

Example: Kalkomey’s Core Domain

At Kalkomey, our Core Domain is the Learning Management System (LMS). This system enables us to deliver state-approved training and certification, a critical function of our business. Here’s how it compares to Supporting Domains: 

Core Domain: Learning Management System (LMS)
- Responsibilities: Manage courses, enrollments, certifications, and compliance reporting.
- Value: Directly tied to state/provincial regulatory compliance and customer success.

Supporting Domain: E-Regulations
- Responsibilities: Maintain hunting and fishing regulations.
- Value: Ensures data accuracy for the LMS but does not drive core innovation.

Supporting Domain: HuntWise
- Responsibilities: Provide tools for hunters and anglers (e.g., GPS maps, planning).
- Value: Enhances user experience but is not the primary revenue driver.

By identifying the LMS as the Core Domain, Kalkomey focuses its resources on maintaining and enhancing its critical functionality.
