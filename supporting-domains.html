Supporting Domains

Supporting Domains complement your Core Domain but are not critical to business differentiation. They provide essential functionality, often using standard solutions or outsourced services.

What are Supporting Domains?

Supporting Domains serve as the backbone for your Core Domain by providing necessary but non-differentiating functionality. Characteristics of Supporting Domains include:

  • Non-Critical: They don’t drive business success directly but are essential for operations.
  • Complementary: They enhance or enable the Core Domain without competing for innovation focus.
  • Efficiency-Oriented: Often implemented using off-the-shelf software or external services.

By managing Supporting Domains efficiently, you can allocate more resources to the Core Domain while ensuring seamless functionality.

How to Handle Supporting Domains

Supporting Domains require a balance of simplicity, reliability, and efficiency. Here are strategies to manage them effectively:

1. Use Off-the-Shelf Solutions

For non-unique functionality, prefer standard tools or SaaS platforms instead of custom development.

Example: Use a third-party analytics tool instead of building one from scratch.

2. Outsource Where Possible

Delegate responsibilities for Supporting Domains to external vendors when it reduces costs and complexity.

Example: Outsource infrastructure hosting to cloud providers like AWS or Azure.

3. Minimize Complexity

Avoid over-engineering Supporting Domains. Aim for simplicity to reduce maintenance overhead.

Example: Use straightforward workflows and integrations for email notifications.

Example: Kalkomey Supporting Domains

At Kalkomey, Supporting Domains like E-Regulations and HuntWise complement the LMS (Core Domain) by providing data and user tools. Here’s how they’re managed: 

Supporting Domain: E-Regulations
- Role: Maintain state and provincial regulations for hunting and fishing.
- Strategy: Streamlined data entry and periodic updates from external sources.

Supporting Domain: HuntWise
- Role: Provide tools like GPS maps, hunting plans, and trophy tracking.
- Strategy: Emphasize user experience while relying on APIs to integrate with LMS and E-Regulations.

Supporting Domain: Marketing Tools
- Role: Manage campaigns and user outreach.
- Strategy: Leverage third-party platforms for email marketing and analytics.

By using efficient solutions for Supporting Domains, Kalkomey can focus resources on optimizing the LMS, our Core Domain.
