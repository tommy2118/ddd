Creating a Shared Language

Learn how to define a shared vocabulary that reflects Kalkomey’s business processes. By aligning on terminology, we ensure consistency, clarity, and effective communication across teams.

Why a Shared Language is Crucial

At Kalkomey, we operate in complex domains such as Certification Management and Compliance Systems. Miscommunication between teams can lead to incorrect assumptions, costly errors, and inefficient workflows. A shared language ensures that:

Everyone is on the Same Page

Developers and domain experts understand terms and concepts in the same way.

Systems Reflect Business Needs

The shared vocabulary becomes embedded in the code, making it easier to maintain and extend.

Collaboration is Seamless

Clear communication fosters trust and improves efficiency during cross-team interactions.

Steps to Create a Shared Language

Gather Key Stakeholders

Bring together domain experts, product managers, and engineers. Use collaborative workshops to identify terms that are critical to Kalkomey’s workflows, such as "certification" or "applicant."

  • Who issues certifications?
  • What does "expired certification" mean?
  • Are there different types of certifications?

Define and Document Terms

Write down clear definitions for each term. For example: 

Certification:
- A document issued to a user upon completing a course, valid for a specific period.
- Includes metadata such as issue date, expiration date, and associated training provider.

Resolve Ambiguities

Clarify terms that could have multiple meanings. For example, does "user" mean a customer or an admin? Use specific terms like "applicant" or "administrator" when appropriate.

Create a Living Document

Store your definitions in a central, accessible location (e.g., Confluence, Notion). Regularly update the document as business processes evolve.

Example: Certification Management

Here’s an example of creating a shared language for a Certification Management workflow:

Certification: - The formal acknowledgment issued upon course completion. Expired Certification: - A certification that is no longer valid, based on its expiration date. Applicant: - A user who has registered for a course but has not yet completed it. Administrator: - A user with permissions to manage course and certification data.
