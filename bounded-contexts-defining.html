Defining Bounded Contexts

Clearly defining the boundaries of a Bounded Context is critical for effective Domain-Driven Design (DDD). By using diagrams, explicit responsibilities, and models, you can ensure that each context is well-defined and understood.

Why Clear Boundaries Matter

Without clear boundaries, overlapping responsibilities and inconsistent terminology can lead to system complexity and confusion. Well-defined boundaries:

Reduce Coupling

Decouple systems by ensuring responsibilities are isolated and independent.

Enhance Team Alignment

Align teams by defining clear scopes for each context.

Support Scalability

Make it easier to grow systems as the business evolves without introducing chaos.

Steps to Define a Bounded Context

Follow these steps to clearly define a Bounded Context:

1. Assign Responsibilities

Define the specific domain logic and business processes that this context handles. Be explicit about what it does and does not handle.

Example: The LMS context manages enrollments and certifications but does not manage hunting plans (HuntWise).

2. Define Terminology

List the terms specific to this context and define their meanings. Avoid reusing terms with different meanings in other contexts.

Example: In the LMS, a "user" is a learner, but in HuntWise, a "user" is a hunter.

3. Map Interactions

Document how this context interacts with others. Use diagrams to show dependencies and communication patterns.

Example: The LMS context relies on E-Regulations for compliance data.

4. Use Context Maps

Create context maps to visualize boundaries and relationships. These diagrams help communicate your design to stakeholders.

Example: LMS and E-Regulations

At Kalkomey, the LMS and E-Regulations have distinct responsibilities but interact to ensure compliance with hunting and fishing laws. Here’s how their boundaries are defined: 

Bounded Context: Learning Management System (LMS)
- Responsibilities: Manage learner enrollments and certifications.
- Terminology: Learner, Certification, Enrollment.
- Interaction: Fetch compliance rules from E-Regulations.

Bounded Context: E-Regulations
- Responsibilities: Maintain and update state/provincial regulations.
- Terminology: Regulation, Rule Change, Season.
- Interaction: Provides regulation data to LMS and HuntWise.

This clear separation ensures each context focuses on its responsibilities without overlapping or creating unnecessary dependencies.
