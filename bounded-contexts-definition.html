What is a Bounded Context?

A Bounded Context is a core concept in Domain-Driven Design (DDD) that defines a clear boundary around a specific domain model. It ensures consistency of language, behavior, and rules within that boundary while isolating it from others.

Understanding Bounded Contexts

A Bounded Context can be thought of as a logical boundary within which a particular domain model applies. This boundary includes:

  • Consistent Terminology: Every term has a single meaning within the context.
  • Defined Rules: Business rules and behaviors are specific to the context.
  • Encapsulation: The domain logic is isolated from other contexts.

Think of a Bounded Context as a self-contained unit, where a domain model is consistent and meaningful, without conflicting with terms or rules in other parts of the system.

Why Bounded Contexts Matter

Bounded Contexts are essential for managing complexity in large systems. Without them, systems can become messy, with terms and behaviors overlapping in unpredictable ways. Here’s what Bounded Contexts help achieve:

Clarity

Each team works within a defined scope, avoiding confusion caused by overloaded terminology.

Isolation

Domain models remain isolated, reducing the risk of unexpected dependencies between systems.

Scalability

Teams can scale their systems independently by focusing on their specific context.

Example: Certification Management

At Kalkomey, the Certification Management system is one Bounded Context. It is responsible for issuing certifications to learners after course completion and ensuring compliance with state or provincial regulations. 

Bounded Context: Certification Management
- Responsible for issuing certifications and managing expiration dates.
- Terms like "certification," "learner," and "expiration date" have specific meanings.
- Does not handle course creation (handled by the LMS context).

This isolation ensures that the Certification Management system can focus on its responsibilities without being affected by changes in unrelated contexts.
