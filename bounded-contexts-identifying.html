Identifying Bounded Contexts

Identifying Bounded Contexts is the first step toward designing clear, maintainable systems. By recognizing natural boundaries in terminology, workflows, and responsibilities, you can build systems that are easy to understand and evolve.

Techniques for Identifying Bounded Contexts

To identify Bounded Contexts, start by analyzing your business domains. Look for areas with distinct responsibilities, terminology, or workflows. These techniques can help:

1. Analyze Terminology

List terms used in your system and identify where their meanings diverge. For example, at Kalkomey:

  • User: Could mean a hunter (HuntWise) or a learner (LMS).
  • Regulation: Relevant to both E-Regulations and Agency Relations but used differently.

2. Examine Workflows

Map out key workflows in your system. Areas with independent workflows often indicate separate contexts. For instance:

  • Certification Issuance in LMS involves learners and courses.
  • Season Updates in E-Regulations deals with hunting laws and agencies.

3. Define Responsibilities

Identify areas with clear, distinct responsibilities. Avoid overlapping responsibilities, as they create unnecessary complexity.

Example: The LMS is responsible for learner enrollments and certifications, while HuntWise focuses on hunting plans and tools.

4. Use Domain Experts

Collaborate with domain experts to understand boundaries that naturally exist in the business. Their insights can reveal where terminology and rules change.

Example: LMS and HuntWise

At Kalkomey, the LMS and HuntWise represent two distinct Bounded Contexts. Here's how their boundaries are defined: 

Bounded Context: Learning Management System (LMS)
- Focus: Manage courses, learner progress, and certifications.
- Key Terms: Learner, Course, Certification, Compliance Report.
- Independent Workflow: Certification issuance process.

Bounded Context: HuntWise
- Focus: Provide tools for hunters (e.g., GPS maps, planning).
- Key Terms: Hunter, Hunt Area, Trophy, Season Plan.
- Independent Workflow: Hunting season planning and tracking.
