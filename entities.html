Entities

Entities are objects with a unique identity that persists over time, even as their attributes change. In Domain-Driven Design, they represent the core concepts of your business.

What are Entities?

An Entity is an object defined by its identity, not by its attributes. It models concepts that are unique and trackable over time, such as a learner, course, or certification in Kalkomey’s systems.

  • Unique Identity: Each Entity is distinguishable by a unique identifier, such as a database ID or a natural key.
  • Stateful: An Entity’s attributes can change over time, but its identity remains constant.
  • Lifecycle: Entities have a lifecycle, from creation to eventual deletion or archival.

In a Bounded Context, Entities encapsulate domain logic that directly relates to their identity and behavior.

Characteristics of Good Entity Design

Designing Entities effectively requires focusing on their identity, lifecycle, and encapsulated behavior. Here are some principles:

1. Unique Identity

Always assign a unique identifier to distinguish an Entity from others, even if they share attributes.

Example: Each Learner in the LMS is identified by a unique ID, even if two learners share the same name.

2. Behavior Encapsulation

Model an Entity’s behaviors and rules directly within the object, avoiding the “anemic domain model” anti-pattern.

Example: A Certification Entity can validate its expiration date or verify completion status.

3. Manage Lifecycles

Ensure the Entity accurately reflects its lifecycle, from creation to updates and eventual deletion.

Example: A Course Entity starts with creation, transitions through updates, and ends with archiving.

Example: Entities in Kalkomey

At Kalkomey, Entities like Learners, Courses, and Certifications represent core domain concepts. Here’s how they’re modeled: 

Entity: Learner
- Attributes: ID, Name, Email, Enrollment Date.
- Behavior: Enroll in a course, track progress, complete certification.

Entity: Course
- Attributes: ID, Title, Description, Start Date.
- Behavior: Add lessons, enroll learners, track completion rates.

Entity: Certification
- Attributes: ID, Learner ID, Course ID, Issue Date, Expiration Date.
- Behavior: Validate expiration, renew certification.

These Entities encapsulate both state and behavior, aligning with business rules while maintaining a unique identity.
